Rap has become overly popular because it's easier. It removes four of the six basic principles of vocality. Vocalists have always made use of key, pitch, melody, sometimes harmony, rhythm and rhyme. Rappers only use rhythm and rhyme. They needn't worry about key, pitch, melody, harmony or any of the other things vocalists typically deal with. Don't get me wrong there are some very talented rappers out there, but essentially it's breaking down vocals to their principal pieces and then only using those that aren't intrinsically visceral. Beats and rhythms are more primal than lofty melodies and complex harmonies. They might carry more "oomph" or emotion or passion, but in doing so they lose the techniques that balance music on that thin passion/technique line. I'm not saying just anyone can rap, but more can rap than can sing. It's more natural and requires less training. So it's more acceptable to the common audience listener.
